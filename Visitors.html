Presentation

La cellule Relations Internationales de LIRIS s'est mise en place à l'automne 2008 afin de conduire le laboratoire, et donc ses équipes, vers la définition d'actions qui pourraient à la fois prendre en compte les incitations des tutelles mais aussi intégrer la volonté des acteurs sur le terrain.

 

Practicalities for people outside the EU

Officially, all non EU visitors must file a security clearance two months prior to their arrival. Get in touch with your host at LIRIS, who will help you file it. It is easy and will also help trigger the organisation of your arrival (getting an office/desk, cards for buildings and restaurants on campus).

For internships: Interns must have an authorisation to be in the lab. It should be filed 2 months in advance, and will help get a VISA if needed. For internships under 3 months, the consulate can deliver it. For internships above 3 months, the student must have his agreement ("convention") signed by all parties (typically university of studies + LIRIS), and the Culture Service from the French Ambassy of the country of residence. Once this is done a security clearance can be delivered. A lot of this paper work will happen through snail mail, so plan ahead !
